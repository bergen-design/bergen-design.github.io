Project Overview

Category: Branding · Web Design · UX Copywriting

Software: Figma, Adobe CC

Services: Brand Strategy, UI/UX Design, Bilingual Copywriting

Maa-min is a ministry helping young believers in Israel (13-25) navigate sex, gender, and identity with biblical truth and grace. I was tasked with creating a complete brand identity and website that would make these sensitive topics approachable and engaging for youth.

The Challenge

Creating a safe, engaging space for Israeli youth to discuss sensitive topics required overcoming several key challenges:

  • Sensitive Subject Matter: Sexuality and faith are often taboo topics that can easily feel heavy or preachy.
  • Youth Engagement: The 13-25 age group has a low tolerance for corporate or academic language.
  • Cultural Nuance: Needed to work in both Hebrew and English while maintaining a consistent tone.
  • Trust Building: Establishing credibility on deeply personal topics with a skeptical young audience.
  • No Existing Brand: Started from scratch with no established visual identity or messaging framework.

The Solution

I developed a complete brand ecosystem that balanced truth with grace, creating a safe space for open conversation:

  • Youth-Centric Brand Voice: Developed a warm, conversational tone that speaks to teens in their language.
  • Visual Identity: Created a clean, modern aesthetic with soft colors and approachable typography.
  • Bilingual Strategy: Crafted messaging that works naturally in both Hebrew and English.
  • No-Shame Philosophy: Built the brand around core messages like "No Shame, Lots of Grace."

Key Features

The final implementation included several strategic elements:

  • Question-Based Content: Sections start with engaging questions to invite curiosity.
  • Bite-Sized Information: Content broken into scannable chunks for better engagement.
  • Safe Space Indicators: Visual and written cues that this is a judgment-free zone.
  • Youth-First Navigation: Simple, intuitive interface that works across devices.
  • Bilingual Support: Seamless language toggling between Hebrew and English.

Design Approach

The visual language was carefully crafted to support the brand's mission:

  • Color Psychology: Soft blues and warm neutrals create a calming, trustworthy environment.
  • Typography: Clean, modern fonts that are highly readable across devices.
  • Iconography: Simple, friendly icons that guide users without overwhelming.
  • Whitespace: Generous spacing to prevent content from feeling heavy or overwhelming.
  • Mobile-First: Designed for the way young people browse - primarily on phones.

The Results

The new brand and website successfully created a welcoming space for young people to explore sensitive topics:

  • Youth Engagement: Content that resonates with the target demographic while maintaining biblical truth.
  • Clear Communication: Complex topics made accessible without being simplistic.
  • Brand Cohesion: Visual and verbal identity that works across all touchpoints.
  • Cultural Relevance: Authentic voice that connects with Israeli youth culture.
  • Safe Environment: Design and copy that encourages open, shame-free conversation.

Wrapping Up

This project demonstrates the power of thoughtful design in creating safe spaces for difficult conversations. By combining youth-friendly aesthetics with biblically-grounded content, we built a brand that's both relevant and respectful.

The success of Maa-min shows how strategic branding and UX can bridge generational and cultural gaps, making profound truths accessible to a new generation.
